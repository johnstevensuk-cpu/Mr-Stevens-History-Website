📐 Edexcel iGCSE — Question Structure Guide

📅 Paper 1 (Cold War & Stalin): Friday 16 May  ·  Paper 2 (Vietnam / Medicine): Thursday 5 June

6-Mark: Interpret

What impression does the author give? LOOK at language choices, selection of evidence, and tone. Analyse HOW the author creates the impression.

8-Mark: Source Utility

WACA provenance + specific quotes + own knowledge that confirms AND challenges. ALL THREE required. Quote exact words — never paraphrase.

16-Mark: Essay

Intro (thesis) → Para 1 (bullet 1) → Para 2 (bullet 2) → Para 3 (own info) → Conclusion. THREE ASPECTS required or marks are capped.

🌐 Cold War: Superpower Relations 1943–72 (Paper 1)

📝 Extended Writing🔍 Source Evaluation💬 Debate🔬 Analysis⚖️ Evaluation💪 ResilienceSDG 16 Peace & Justice🔍 AI Critical Thinking⚖️ AI Ethics

The ideological conflict between the USA and USSR from 1945 shaped the modern world. Key crises: Berlin Blockade (1948–49), Hungarian Uprising (1956), Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) — 13 days to potential nuclear war. LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE on every source question. Paper 1: Friday 16 May.

🌐 Activity 1: LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE — CIA Cuba Photograph
  • LOOK: Describe exactly what you can see. Without analysts' labels, structures are almost unidentifiable.
  • CHECK (WACA): Who took this (CIA)? When? For whom? What was the CIA's aim?
  • INFER: What does this photograph IMPLY about Soviet intentions?
  • JUDGE: 'This source is useful for understanding [X] because... however it is limited because...'
🔍 Activity 2: WACA — Churchill's Iron Curtain Speech
  • Churchill was no longer Prime Minister (lost 1945 election). Yet Truman sat beside him on the platform.
  • How does Truman's presence affect our WACA analysis of the speech?
📜 Primary Source — Winston Churchill, Fulton, Missouri, 5 March 1946
The Iron Curtain Speech
“From Stettin in the Baltic to Trieste in the Adriatic, an iron curtain has descended across the Continent. Behind that line lie all the capitals of Central and Eastern Europe.”
WWho?
AAudience?
CContext?
AAim?
  • WACA: Who gave this speech? Where and when? Audience — note: Truman was on the platform! What was Churchill trying to achieve?
  • Churchill was no longer PM. Yet Truman sat beside him. Does Truman's presence give Churchill's words US government backing?
  • How useful is this source for understanding the origins of the Cold War?

☭ Stalin's Russia 1924–53 (Paper 1)

🔍 Source Evaluation📝 Extended Writing🔬 Analysis⚖️ Evaluation💪 ResilienceSDG 10 InequalitiesSDG 16 Peace & Justice⚖️ AI Ethics

Stalin rose to power transforming the USSR through Five-Year Plans, collectivisation, the Great Purges and a cult of personality. The Holodomor (1932–33) was deliberately engineered. The Great Terror (1936–38) used the NKVD, show trials and the Gulag. Apply LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE to Stalin's official portrait. Paper 1: Friday 16 May.

🔍 Activity 1: LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE — Stalin's Portrait
  • LOOK: List FIVE specific visual choices — clothing, expression, pose, lighting, gaze. What does each communicate?
  • CHECK: Who commissioned this? For whom? With what aim? Note: made DURING the Great Terror and Holodomor.
  • JUDGE: The GAP between this image and the Gulag/Holodomor reality IS the historical evidence. Discuss.
⚖️ Activity 2: Terror AND Propaganda — Both Were Needed
  • TERROR (Unit 3): NKVD, Show Trials and Gulag — with specific examples, dates and numbers
  • PROPAGANDA (Unit 4): cult of personality, censorship and Stakhanovism
  • Why did Stalin need BOTH? Why was terror alone not sufficient?

🚁 The Vietnam War 1954–75 (Paper 2)

📝 Extended Writing🌐 Global Perspectives🔍 Source Evaluation⚖️ Evaluation🔗 Making Connections💪 ResilienceSDG 16 Peace & Justice🔍 AI Critical Thinking

America's longest war — from French colonial collapse through escalation, the Tet Offensive, and the fall of Saigon in 1975. TET OFFENSIVE KEY DISTINCTION: US/South Vietnam won MILITARILY — but it was a devastating POLITICAL DEFEAT. Apply LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE to the Vietnam composite image. Paper 2: Thursday 5 June.

🔍 Activity 1: LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE — Vietnam Composite
  • LOOK: Study the composite image. Identify what each visual element represents — US helicopters, Vietnamese fighters, civilians, anti-war protests.
  • CHECK: Who produced this composite? When? For whom? What are the LIMITATIONS of a composite image?
  • JUDGE: 'This source is useful for understanding [X] because... however it is limited because...'
⚔️ Activity 2: The Tet Offensive — The KEY DISTINCTION
  • TET KEY DISTINCTION: US forces won militarily — but it was a POLITICAL DEFEAT. Explain that paradox clearly.
  • Walter Cronkite declared the war 'a stalemate' on 27 February 1968. He was trusted by 73% of Americans. Why does that statistic matter?

💉 Changes in Medicine c.1848–1948 (Paper 2)

📊 Data Analysis📝 Extended Writing🔬 Research Skills💡 Problem Solving🔗 Making Connections💪 ResilienceSDG 1 No PovertySDG 3 Good Health🛠️ AI Tools

Apply LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE to the penicillin image above. Fleming discovered it in 1928 — BEFORE WW2. Florey and Chain developed it into a usable antibiotic by 1940. Note the 12-YEAR GAP. Beveridge's Five Giants (exact): Want, Disease, Ignorance, Squalor, Idleness. Paper 2: Thursday 5 June.

🔍 Activity 1: LOOK-CHECK-INFER-JUDGE — Penicillin Image
  • LOOK: What does each stage shown in the image represent? Name each stage.
  • INFER: What does the 12-year gap between discovery (1928) and usable antibiotic (1940) imply about WW2's ACTUAL role?
  • JUDGE: 'This source is useful for understanding [X] because... however the 12-year gap tells us...'
📋 Activity 2: Beveridge's Five Giants
  • For EACH Giant (Want, Disease, Ignorance, Squalor, Idleness): define it precisely, give ONE specific real-world example in 1942, explain what the NHS proposed to address.
